Medical Intern

“I don't know what I'm doing, I'm not old enough to use a scalpel without parental supervision.”

The Medical Intern is a learner role to acquire the basics of applying medicine, using chemicals and medical tools. They answer to the rest of the medical staff, especially to the CMO. 

The job of a Medical Intern are quite simple, though can be challenging at times. You are expected to assist medical doctors and learn from them, whether it is to treat patients by applying proper topicals and chemicals, do surgical operations, or help drag bodies to the morgue when nothing else can be done to save them. 

Don't be scared to ask for anything involving the job, medical doctors and the CMO are more than happy to teach you when they have the time to.

 

Equipment

All medical personnel needs to be familiar with the equipment at their disposal. Once you understand them, any (most) injuries can be treated with relative ease.

 

Treating Patients

As a Medical Intern, you should learn how to treat different kind of injuries. There are many kinds of them, like brute, burn, toxin, and airloss damage. Each can be further divided into more detailed injuries.
