Role Overview: Cleaning, Chaos, and Quiet Heroism

The Janitor is the unsung backbone of the station. Though part of the Service department, your work spans the entire facility — corridors, engineering bays, bars, and sometimes crime scenes. Your job sounds simple: keep the station clean, functional, and safe. In practice, it’s a juggling act of vigilance, creative problem-solving, and quiet resilience. You’ll be blamed for other people’s messes and praised only by absence.

Janitors mop floors, replace lights, remove graffiti, and haul away trash. But you'll also erase evidence, discover corpses, and intervene in the wake of clown-induced disasters. A well-timed mop swing or perfectly placed sign can mean the difference between order and chaos.

Daily Duties

Core Responsibilities

  • Mopping: Use mop and water to remove puddles—blood, lube, vomit, whatever it is Science left behind. Replace dirty water frequently.
  • Graffiti Removal: Spray and mop offensive or out-of-place graffiti. Consider artistic merit first—especially mime art.
  • Trash Collection: Use trash bags for loose garbage. Use the trash cart for bulk jobs. Encourage proper bin usage.
  • Light Replacement: Fix burnt-out lights using the Light Replacer and bulbs/tubes.
  • Wet Floor Sign Deployment: Always mark wet areas before mopping. Most will ignore it—but you’ll be in the clear.
  • Side Jobs: Unclog toilets, place traps, clean debris from fights, or help after explosions. Expect variety.

Responding to Requests

Stay tuned to your Service radio. Departments will ping you for cleanups.

  • Medbay: Blood and biohazards. Clean quickly and unobtrusively.
  • Science: Unidentified fluids, spills, and residue.
  • Bar: Drinks, vomit, smashed glasses—standard chaos.

Late Shifts & Emergencies

  • If joining late: gear up fast, the station is probably a mess.
  • During emergencies: clear main paths, handle blood spills, dispose of bodies or hazards if Medbay is overwhelmed.

Advanced Cleaning: Going Beyond the Mop

Expanding Your Role

  • Know the Station: Learn paths, maintenance tunnels, and department layouts.
  • Resource Redistribution: Deliver steel, glass, or cloth from salvage to departments that need it.
  • Dead Animals: Give to the Chef or Cargo for processing.
  • Use the Recycler: Keep your rounds clean and profitable.

Salvage Operations

  • Crowbar: For opening doors when power fails.
  • Wrench/Screwdriver: Unbolt and move furniture, racks, or light fixtures. Useful for material recovery.
  • Networking: Trade scrap for goodwill or tools. Build alliances with Engineers, Quartermasters, or Scientists.

Hazard Awareness

  • Crime Scenes: Do not clean blood or evidence without clearance from Security.
  • Dangerous Fluids: If you don’t know what it is — don’t touch it. Ping Science or Security.
  • Self-Defense: Use your mop as a weapon if needed (Z to wield). Surprisingly effective.

Best Practices & Survival Tips

  • Always wear galoshes.
  • Don’t hoard. Share or recycle surplus.
  • Label dangerous areas with signage.
  • Prioritize major corridors and Arrivals.
  • Help lost crewmates or drag wounded to Medbay.
  • Clean before someone slips. Clean after someone slips.

Supporting the Crew

  • Assist Medbay by removing blood or cleaning treatment rooms.
  • Provide Engineering with salvage.
  • Alert Security to suspicious finds (e.g., bloody knives, bodies).
  • Help maintain public areas like the Bar, Arrivals, and Bridge access.
  • In chaos, be the one clearing paths, guiding people, and keeping morale high through order.

Janitorial Code & Station Culture

Ethics

  • Don’t remove evidence without cause.
  • Don’t erase meaningful art unless necessary.
  • Don’t escalate clown rivalry unless you're ready to commit.
  • Ask questions. Learn from older janitors.

Roleplay Hooks

  • Build a janitorial persona — silent guardian, proud custodian, mop-wielding monk.
  • Narrate your cleaning or speak in slogans.
  • Establish janitorial territory.
  • Rival or ally with the Clown, HoP, or AI.

The Legend of the Janitor

Great Janitors become myths. They’re whispered about — cleaners who never miss a puddle, who maintain Arrivals during a cult round, who wield a mop like a claymore. If nobody knows your name but the floors are always gleaming, you’ve done it right.

Final Thoughts

Nobody thanks the Janitor when the station is clean — but everyone complains when it isn’t. You are unthanked, unarmed, and often unnoticed — but essential.

Clean with pride. Clean with purpose. And never underestimate the power of a wet floor sign.
