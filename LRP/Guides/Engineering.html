Basics to Power Transportation and Storage

This section discusses the types of power as well as transforming and storing it.

 

Voltage Groups and Transformation

There represent 3 voltage groups of electricity: Low (LV), medium (MV), and high (HV). 

(Combine the above into a single figure, bruh.)

It is to note, these wires are not directly interchangeable and need to be transformed. Doing so requires one of two machines:

  • Substation (HV ↔ MV)
  • APC (MV ↔ LV)

 

In essence, most machinery found on the station run on LV, whilst most of power production produces HV. Hence, the produced power needs to be transformed down for station use.

 

Power Storage

Substations and APCs buffer buffer a small amount of power. However, for storage of bulk power requires the use of a superconducting magnetic energy storage (SMES).
