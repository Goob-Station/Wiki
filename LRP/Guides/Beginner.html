Welcome to Goobstation! You and your fellow crewmembers are now proud employees of Nanotrasen, stationed aboard a barely-functional orbital facility. Your goal: do your job, survive your shift, and maybe have fun in the process.

Before You Start

  • 📜 Read the server rules. Press F1 in-game to review them at any time.
  • ❓ Lost or confused? Ask questions:
    • In-game, via radio or direct chat.
    • On Discord (#help channel; no spoilers).
    • Or use Ahelp for admin support (Escape → Admin Help).

Getting Started

When you first join, you’ll pick (or be assigned) a job. Not sure what to choose? We recommend:

  • Passenger – You have no duties and zero responsibility. Perfect for learning controls and exploring.

Set “Passenger” to high priority, all others to “Never”. Walk around, chat, rummage in your backpack, and maybe don’t stand next to explosives.

If you join late, wait at the arrivals shuttle. It’ll dock automatically and bring you aboard. Look for a map or follow the screaming.

How to Not Get Banned Instantly

  • Do not kill, attack, or grief people unless you're an Antagonist.
  • Do not break into secure areas or steal gear “for fun”.
  • Do not use out-of-character (OOC) info in-character (IC), and vice versa. That's called metagaming.
  • Don’t be a jerk. You’re here to create fun for others, not just yourself.

What Is Roleplaying?

You're playing a character. Stay in character (IC) at all times. Your crewmates are coworkers, not enemies. The station is your workplace, not your playground. Follow SOP and act like you're part of the crew.

OOC means anything outside the game world: Discord, OOC chat, etc. Don’t talk about the current round there. Don’t act on info your character wouldn’t reasonably know.

✅ Do❌ Don’t
  • Stay in character
  • Talk to people, ask for help
  • React as your character would
  • Discuss the round in OOC
  • Attack people without cause
  • Use meta info to gain advantage

So You Want to Be Useful

Once you know the controls, try:

  • Service jobs – Like Bartender, Chef, or Janitor. Great for roleplay and low risk.
  • Cargo Technician – Explore the station and deliver gear.
  • Medical Intern, Technical Assistant, or Research Assistant – Shadow professionals and learn complex systems.

Antagonists and Chaos

Some players are picked to be Antagonists – saboteurs, assassins, or worse. You can volunteer for these roles, but they're assigned randomly each round.

If you’re picked, you’ll see a message. Press C to check. If it says “Crew Aligned”, you’re not an antagonist. Don’t act like one.

Being an antagonist does not mean “go on a killing spree”. Follow your objectives. Be subtle, clever, or dramatic — but don’t ruin the round for everyone.

How to React

Suspect foul play? Contact Security. Avoid vigilante justice unless you're certain. And remember: even antags deserve trials… unless they're nukies. Then all bets are off.

Don't Self-Antag

That means pretending to be a villain when you’re not one. Breaking into places, stealing gear, attacking crew, hoarding weapons — that’s not okay unless the game says you’re an antag. You’ll get banned.

Still Unsure?

Ask. We don’t bite. Unless we’re spiders. But then it's IC.
